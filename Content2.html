-Nickname: Philippines’ Last Frontier

-Palawan has 4 airports: Coron Busuanga, El Nido, Puerto Princesa, and San Vicente

-Average temperature: 84°F (29°C)

-Best off-the-beaten-path: Balabac and Taytay

-Best Island Hopping tour: El Nido Tour A

-Wettest month: August

-Palawan has been voted “Best Island in the World” several times.

-The Tubbataha Reefs is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a top diving destination.

-El Nido is famous for its crystal-clear waters, dramatic limesto-ne cliffs, and lagoons.

-Palawan has 1,780 islands and islets.

-Cuyo Island is one of the oldest Spanish settlements in the Philippines.

-Malampaya Sound is known as the “Amazon of the Seas” due to its rich marine life.

-Palawan is home to the Philippine Pangolin, one of the world’s most endangered animals.

-Balabac in southern Palawan has beaches with pinkish sand.

-Palawan’s waters are habitat to dugongs (sea cows), especially in Busuanga.

-Capital: Puerto Princes

-Weather: tropical climate with a dry season and a wet season.

-Most popular destinations: El Nido and Coron

-Best tour: Underground River Cruise

-Best month to travel: April

-Best diving: Coron and Tubbataha Reef

-It is home to the Puerto Princesa Underground River, a New 7 Wonder of Nature.

-Palawan has 600+ butterfly species and 200+ bird species.

-Kayangan Lake in Coron is known as the cleanest lake in the Philippines.

-The Tagbanua Tribe is one of the oldest ethnic groups living in Palawan.

-Palawan has prehistoric caves with ancient drawings, like those in Tabon Caves.

-You can find the Palawan Bearcat, a rare and unique tree-dwelling mammal.

-The St. Paul Mountain Range hides underground rivers, waterfalls, and caves.

-Some beaches have bioluminescent plankton that glow at night.

-Honda Bay offers island-hopping adventures with starfish-filled beaches.
